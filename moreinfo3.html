    Screw Water Turbine

    A water screw turbine, also known as an Archimedes Screw Turbine (AST), is a device that uses the weight of flowing water to generate electricity: How it works Water enters the top of the turbine's shaft, causing the screw blades to rotate and the water to fall to a lower level. Benefits ASTs can be used in low-head hydropower sites, where water flows are low and heads are very low. They are also fish-friendly, as their low rotation speed reduces negative impacts on aquatic life.
